Preposição: Dado um grafo $G = (V,E)$ qualquer com n vertices, $G$ ou complemento de $G$ é conexo:

\begin{proof}
If $G$ is connected then we are done, so suppose $G$ is disconnected. Choose $u,v \in V$ such that there is no $(u,v)-path$ in graph $G$. Let $\bar G $ be the complement of $G$ and $A$, $B$ be sets, such that, $A=\{s \in G \colon \exists (s,v)-path\}$ and $B = \{t \in G \colon \nexists (t,v)-path\}$ note that $A\cup v=V\setminus B$ and $\{t,v\} \notin E$ for all $t \in B$ otherwise would exist a $(t,v)-path$, so $\{t,v\} \in E'$ for all $t \in B$. For every $s \in A$ there is no $(u,s)-path$ otherwise would be a $(u,v)-path$ using vertex $s$, so $\{u,s\} \in E'$ for all $s \in A$ and clearly $\{u,v\} \in E'$ so we proved $A,B$ and $v$ are connected in graph $G'$ and once $A\cup B\cup v = V$ we are done.
\end{proof}
